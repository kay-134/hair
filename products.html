Let's Talk About Product Basics!

Not all hair products are the same!

Although you might have seen a couple of those 3 or even 4 in one products at the super market or even your local beauty supply store I personally believe that, when it comes to hair products for curly hair, one singular item cannot accomplish multiple crucial tasks at the same time.

Starting out in my own hair journey, I saw how much healthier and cleaner my hair was when I was intentional about the different steps in the cleaning/styling process and I want to break down what each product is used for & recommend products that haave worked for me!

Shampoo

Definition

Shampoo: Used for cleaning hair by removing dirt, sweat, and various other environmental pollutants.

When Do I Use This?

I apply this first when I'm washing my hair! This allows all the gunk to get out so I can have an easier time detangling & styling. Sometimes I like to let the shampoo sit on my hair for a bit while before I wash it off, massaging it into the scalp to deeply clean my hair

Conditioner

Definition

Conditioner: Applied after shampoo, this product is designed to seal in moisture and provide nutrients to the hair, making it soft and manageable.

When Do I Use This?

I apply the conditioner after shampoo to lock in moisture into my hair so it isn't stripped of all the oils/moisture needed to have clean and healthy hair! Think of conditioner as the lotion you to your hands after you wash them!

Leave-In Conditioner

Definition

Leave in Conditioners: Applied after shampooing and conditioning to keep the hair hydrated, detangled, and protected throughout the day.

When Do I Use This?

I apply this either after I wash out the conditioner and am ready to style my hair or when my hair is clean but I want to switch up the style so I can apply it to wet hair too!

Additional Products

Castor Oil & Styling Gels

Castor Oil: Applied to roots before styling to also lock in moisture and hair texture, it's often credited to help with hair growth as well.

Styling Gels: Used to add some extra pizzaz to a hair style, sets baby hairs/flyaways and can prevent them from moving.

When Do I Use This?

I use both of these products after I wash my hair & detangle it with a leave-in conditioner, both aren't 100% necessary but are both products I frequently use.
